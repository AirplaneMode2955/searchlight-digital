5-Step GEO Improvement Plan — Prioritized by Score Impact
01
⚡ Quick Win
Implement Organization and FAQPage Schema Markup
High Impact
Finding: No Organization, LocalBusiness, or FAQPage schema detected on any page. This is the single highest-leverage GEO fix — Gemini and Perplexity pull directly from structured data in AI-generated answers, and a missing Organization schema means LLMs cannot confidently identify what Acme Consulting is or cite it accurately.
  • Add Organization schema to the homepage with consistent name, URL, logo, founding year, service area, and social profile links. This is the foundation — every other schema type builds on it.
  • Add FAQPage schema to your top 3 service pages using the questions clients actually ask. Use verbatim question phrasing as the schema Q field — these become direct LLM citation sources for category queries.
  • Validate all schema using Google's Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results) before publishing. Fix any errors flagged — broken schema is treated as absent by crawlers.
02
⚡ Quick Win
Add Attributed Testimonials and a Visible Review Aggregate
High Impact
Finding: Brand Authority scored 8/15 — testimonials on the site are generic with no names or company attributions. LLMs treat unattributed testimonials as non-evidence. Two qualifying E-E-A-T signals exist, but named case studies or attributed testimonials are absent, which triggers the Claude penalty and suppresses trust signals across all platforms.
  • Convert existing generic testimonials to attributed quotes: name, title, company, and one measurable outcome (e.g., "reduced operating costs by 22% in 6 months"). Real attribution turns a testimonial into a citable fact.
  • Add a visible review aggregate badge (Google or Clutch) to the homepage footer and service pages. Even a widget showing "4.8 stars · 24 reviews" signals third-party validation to LLMs that process on-page content.
  • Add Review/AggregateRating schema once the widget is in place. This surfaces the rating directly in AI-generated summaries about the firm.
03
⚡ Quick Win
Restructure the About Page as a Machine-Readable Entity Profile
Medium Impact
Finding: The About page exists but lacks the structured entity signals LLMs use to identify and describe a firm — no founding year stated, no named principals with credentials, no service area explicitly defined, and no external validation anchors (associations, certifications, awards).
  • Add a "Who We Are" section with: founding year, named principals with titles and credentials, explicit service area (geography + industry), and the firm's core differentiator stated in one sentence. These are the facts LLMs cite when answering "who is Acme Consulting?"
  • List every association membership, certification, or award the firm holds — even chamber of commerce membership counts. External validation anchors are direct citation triggers for ChatGPT and Claude.
  • Link to LinkedIn profiles from the About page and ensure those profiles list Acme Consulting consistently. LinkedIn is indexed in LLM training data and cross-references entity signals from the website.
04
🎯 Big Picture
Build a Third-Party Citation Profile Across Industry Directories
High Impact
Finding: Citation & Third-Party Presence scored 11/20 — the second-lowest dimension. Only 1–2 directory listings are detectable on the site. This is the direct cause of the Grok, ChatGPT, and Perplexity penalties. Competitors with 2–3x more citations consistently outrank in category queries — external mentions are a primary signal AI systems use to validate brand legitimacy.
  • Get listed on every relevant consulting directory within 30 days: Clutch.co (the most LLM-indexed consulting directory), G2, Expertise.com, SCORE mentor network, and your local chamber of commerce. Each listing is an independent citation that LLMs treat as a separate brand validation signal.
  • Pursue one authoritative press mention per quarter — a single citation in a business journal (local or trade) does more for LLM visibility than 20 directory listings. Pitch your firm's niche expertise angle to regional business press and industry newsletters.
  • Answer 4–6 questions per month on Quora and Reddit in /r/smallbusiness and /r/consulting. These platforms are heavily indexed in LLM training data. Include your firm name and credentials naturally — not promotional, but substantive answers that establish topical authority.
05
🎯 Big Picture
Build an Educational Content Library Targeting Category and Problem Queries
High Impact
Finding: Zero visibility across all 4 category queries and all 3 problem/solution queries tested — 7 of 12 total. The blog exists with 2–3 posts but none directly target the query language clients use when researching consulting services. LLMs can't recommend a firm they've never "read" answering the specific question being asked.
  • Write 4–6 long-form articles (1,000+ words) using the exact question format as H1: "When should I hire a strategy consultant?", "How to choose the right consulting firm for your business", "What does a business consultant actually do — and what does it cost?" These map directly to the High Priority queries in the table above.
  • Build a dedicated FAQ page at /faq with 15–20 Q&As mirroring how people ask AI tools about consulting services. Add FAQPage schema to this page. This becomes a standing LLM citation source that compounds over time.
  • Establish a consistent publishing cadence of 2 articles per month. Topical authority builds from volume and consistency — a single article won't move the needle, but 12 targeted articles over 6 months will measurably improve category query visibility.
Methodology

GEO scores in this report are inferred from web presence signals — not live API queries to each LLM platform. This is intentional: the signals that predict LLM visibility (entity consistency, content authority, citation volume, structured data, and review presence) are the same signals you can directly control and improve. A live query shows a snapshot; this methodology shows you why you rank where you do and what to fix.

Overall scoring uses a weighted composite of 6 dimensions: Entity Clarity (20pts), Content Signals (20pts), Citation Presence (20pts), Schema & Technical Structure (15pts), Brand Authority (15pts), and Topical Coverage (10pts). Per-platform scores are adjusted from the baseline using each LLM's known signal weighting tendencies — not live query results.

Competitor scores are estimated using the same methodology applied to publicly available signals. These are directional benchmarks, not exact measurements. Report prepared by IndexForge LLM.
